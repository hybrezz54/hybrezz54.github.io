Nature's Tranquility

Pinehurst's serene and harmonious environment soothes the human mind. Some of the variety of fauna and flora are described below, respectively.


Grey Squirrel

Diet: Acorns, nuts, berries, fungi. Will eat insects and rodents if they need to. Breeding: twice a year; once in december to february and may to June. Homes: Located on treetops and in hollow tree trunks. It is usually constructed of moss, thistledown, dried grass, and feathers.

Carolina Locust

A short horned grasshopper who eats leaves and grasses and travel in swarms.
They lay up to 60 eggs at once and shed 8 times until they're adults.

White Tailed Deer

Type: Mammal, Diet: Herbivore, Average life span in captivity: 6 to 14 years
Size: 6 to 7.75 ft (1.8 to 2.4 m), Weight: 110 to 300 lbs (50 to 136 kg), Group name: Herd

Green Tree Frog

Diet: The green tree frog eats small insects and other invertebrates. Characteristics: The green treefrog is 1¼ to 2½ inches long. It is bright green, greenish gray or yellow green. Found in marshes; wet prairies; cypress swamps and along the edges of lakes, ponds and streams.

Red Wolf

North America's rarest canine, these animals live in packs of five to eight and stand 26 inches tall at the shoulder. Mates live with each other for life and family packs spread out over an area of 25-50 square miles. A typical diet consists of deer, racoons, rabbits and various rodents.
